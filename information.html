Introduction

Level 2 Games Design @ NWSLC

Course Overview:
Our Games design course it tailored to the meet the requirements of the modern games industry. Providing students the best diverse skill set to ensure their success! Here some key areas covered within the course:

1. Video Editing
Learn Video editing is a crucial skill set for creating captivating games trailers, cinematics, and promotional content.

2. Digital photography skills
Unlock the power of digital photography with applications in game development, from reference gathering to texture creation, these skills are needed to enhance visual game elements.

3. Digital Image Manipulation
Dive into the world of digital image manipulation, using tools such as Photoshop, creating stunning game assets, promotional materials, and advanced editing techniques.

4. Sound production
Understand the principles of sound production design. Learn to edit, record, and mix audio to create immersive gaming experiences.

5. Filming Processes
Explore the fundamentals of filming! From Cinematic sequences to motion capture, gain insight into bringing narrative to life within the gaming world. Students By completing this course, students are equipped with creative skills that prepares them for diverse roles within the Film and games industry. Skill sets: Games design knowledge, development, marketing and more! Ready to embark on your own journey to exciting worlds created with multimedia skills for gaming? Start your career here and unlock your potential!

Equipment and Additional Costs

In this role, your course materials are a few essential Items you’ll need to ensure that you’re fully prepared!! Here’s what you’ll require:

1.Stationery
Here is the basic kit such as notepads, pens, pencils, and any other writing materials you prefer!

2.USB Memory Stick
A memory stick is essential for storing and transferring your digital projects for the classroom! This will allow you to access your work from various computers and share with the students.

3.Diary
Keep track of deadlines, class schedules, and project timelines. It’s crucial for staying organized!

4.External Hard Drive (optional)
While not essential, an external hard drive that’s formatted for multiple software engines provides easy access to files! Example formats: Mac, Windows and more. This additional storage space helps safeguard your work in case of computer malfunctions or data loss. Please note that these items are not included in employment apart from a laptop! However, these items will support your teaching/learning experience to contribute to your success!

If you have any questions about the equipment requirements or additional, feel free to reach out to us for clarification! We’re here to support you throughout your educational journey.

Help and Advice At NWSLC, we are dedicated to ensuring you make the most of your time at the college while upholding EDI– Equality, diversity, Inclusion, and the British values. That’s why we offer a range of services:

At NWSLC, safeguarding is everyone’s responsibility, to ensure that you enjoy your classroom teachings in a safe and secure environment, reflecting the EDI and the British value of democracy rule of law. Your well-being as well as the students is out top priority, and we are committed to providing a supportive and inclusive atmosphere for all students. If you have any safeguarding concerns or require support, please don’t hesitate to contact our Safeguarding Team. Your safety and welfare are out utmost priority.

1. Presence on campus
Our safeguarding team is always present on campus, providing a visible and accessible point of contact for anyone who may have concerns about their safety or safety of others.

2. Promoting safety and security
Actively promoting safety and security measures across our campuses, including clear signage, emergency procedures and regular safety drills.

3. Reporting Concerns
If you have any safeguarding concerns or issues, whether related to yourself or others, our safeguarding Team is here to help. You can approach them in person, by phone, via email, and they will get your concerns addressed promptly and confidentially.

4. Training and Awareness
Staff members receive regular training on safeguarding procedures and protocols to ensure they are equipped to recognize and respond to safeguarding concerns effectively.

5. Partnership Working
We work closely with external agencies and partners to ensure that a coordinated approach to safeguarding. Therefore, collaborating with the local authorities, charities, and support services, we can provide the support to those who need it.
